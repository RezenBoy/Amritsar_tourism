About Amritsar

Explore the historical journey of Amritsar through time.

The Origin of Amritsar

Amritsar was founded in 1577 by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru. Centered around the sacred Amrit Sarovar, the city soon became the spiritual nucleus of Sikhism. Guru Arjan Dev began the construction of Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple), a symbol of equality and peace.

British Colonial Period

During British rule, Amritsar was a hub of resistance. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919 shook the nation and intensified the freedom movement. This dark chapter marks Amritsar’s deep involvement in India’s independence journey.

Modern Amritsar

Today, Amritsar is a global spiritual destination. It thrives with tourism, trade, and culture — from the Golden Temple to street food, from textile exports to cross-border diplomacy at Wagah. A perfect blend of history and progress.
