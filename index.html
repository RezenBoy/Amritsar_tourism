About

Amritsar Sahib, founded in 1577 by Guru Ram Das, is a historic city in Punjab, India, known for its spiritual and cultural significance. It is home to the Golden Temple, the holiest Sikh shrine, built by Guru Arjan Dev and surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar.

The city witnessed the tragic Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919 and is also famous for the Wagah Border ceremony...





Maps

Amritsar Sahib is located in the northwestern state of Punjab, India, near the India-Pakistan border. The city lies at approximately 31.63°N latitude and 74.87°E longitude, covering an area of about 170 square kilometers. It is well-connected by National Highway NH-3 and has a railway station linking it to major Indian cities.

The Golden Temple, the heart of the city, is centrally located, surrounded by key landmarks like Jallianwala Bagh and the Partition Museum. The Wagah Border lies around 28 km west, serving as the only road crossing between India and Pakistan.

Amritsar’s map features a mix of old city lanes and modern infrastructure, with bustling markets, historical sites, and green spaces. Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport connects it globally.
